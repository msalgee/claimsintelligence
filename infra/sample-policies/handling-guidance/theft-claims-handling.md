# Section 12 — Auto Theft Claims Handling

This section provides procedural guidance for handling auto theft claims from initial intake through settlement or recovery, with specific attention to same-day and near-term recovery scenarios, suspicious circumstance investigation, and SIU referral criteria.

## 12.1 Initial Intake and Police Report Verification

At first notice of loss (FNOL), obtain from the insured:
- Exact time and location of last known vehicle location before discovery of theft
- Exact date and time theft was discovered
- Whether police report has been filed; if yes, obtain police report number and jurisdiction
- Description of vehicle condition at time of theft (locked, windows up, contents, any visible damage)
- Keys in insured's possession (original, spare, fob battery condition)
- Whether vehicle was parked in secure or open lot

Before proceeding past FNOL acknowledgment, verify that a police report exists for all auto theft claims. If the insured has not filed, advise them to file immediately and advise that investigation and claim processing will be held pending receipt of the police report. Document the police report case number and jurisdiction in the claim file and request a certified copy.

Do not accept a police report filed more than 72 hours after discovery unless the insured can demonstrate reasonable cause for delay (hospitalization, travel, isolated rural area with delayed sheriff availability). Delayed reports are a fraud indicator and trigger SIU evaluation.

## 12.2 NICB and NCIC Checks

Within 24 hours of FNOL for any theft claim:
- Submit the vehicle VIN to the National Insurance Crime Bureau (NICB) database to confirm the vehicle is not flagged as previously reported stolen or recovered without notice.
- Confirm vehicle VIN matches the insured's policy declarations.
- If the vehicle appears in NICB as previously reported stolen in the past 60 months without resolution, deny the claim and refer to SIU immediately (likely staged theft or title washing scenario).

Query the National Crime Information Center (NCIC) auto theft database (via local law enforcement) to confirm the vehicle is currently reported stolen and obtain any recovery status updates. Refresh NCIC status weekly while the vehicle remains unreported.

## 12.3 Recovered Vehicle Workflow (Same-Day and 48-Hour Recovery)

**Critical:** When a stolen vehicle is recovered within 48 hours of FNOL, settlement and investigation timelines are compressed. See Section 16 (Same-Day Recovery Procedure) for full protocol. This subsection covers the decision tree to route to Section 16.

When notification is received that the reported-stolen vehicle has been recovered:
- Confirm recovery date and time, recovery location (police impound, private lot, recovered by insured, recovered by police)
- If recovery is same-day or within 48 hours of loss discovery, immediately invoke Section 16 protocol and assign a joint inspection resource.
- If recovery is 48 to 72 hours after loss, initiate Section 16 protocol but allow compressed investigation timeline (14 calendar days to settlement) only if vehicle damage is minor and no suspicious circumstances exist at inspection.
- If recovery is beyond 72 hours, treat as recovered but investigate for staged theft (extended absent time, condition inconsistencies, mileage discrepancies).

## 12.4 Unrecovered Vehicle Workflow (30-Day Rule and Total Loss Path)

For vehicles not recovered within 72 hours of FNOL, follow the unrecovered-vehicle investigation and settlement process:

**Days 1–10:** Confirm vehicle is still reported active in NCIC. If NCIC shows recovery without notice to Contoso, verify recovery location immediately and route to Section 16 (Recovered Procedure). If still active, maintain investigation status.

**Days 10–20:** If no recovery has been reported via police, insurance industry database, or salvage pool notification, conduct expanded search:
- Contact area salvage pools and collision shops within 50 miles to request notification if vehicle arrives for sale or parts.
- Contact local towing services in the theft jurisdiction and surrounding areas.
- Review police incident reports for mentions of recovered matching-description vehicles in case number lookup.

**Days 20–30:** If vehicle remains unrecovered, prepare for total-loss settlement:
- Order industry valuation (CCC ONE primary) to determine ACV of the stolen vehicle as of date of theft.
- Prepare settlement offer based on ACV less applicable deductible. See Section 15 (Total-Loss Valuation Procedure) for ACV determination details.
- Coordinate with any lienholder or lessor on settlement timing.

**Day 30 and Beyond:** If the insured has not requested settlement and investigation remains open, place the claim in "awaiting resolution" status. Auto-close the claim 60 days post-FNOL if no recovery or additional documentation is received, notifying the insured in writing that the claim is administratively closed but may be reopened if recovery occurs.

## 12.5 Key and VIN Validation

For any theft claim, obtain and verify:
- All keys (original, spare, fob) that were in the insured's possession at time of theft. Obtain serial numbers if available or photograph each key with date stamp.
- Exact VIN from the policy declarations page and from the vehicle title.
- VIN from the police report and confirm match with policy.
- If any VIN mismatch exists between title, policy, and police report, flag immediately for SIU review before proceeding past FNOL.

High-risk key scenarios (requiring escalation to SIU):
- No spare key exists and insured cannot produce evidence of ever having lost a spare.
- Keys were stored in the vehicle at time of theft (garage door opener, house key, etc., creating access risk).
- Insured reports losing keys 1–7 days before theft.
- Insured reports multiple sets of keys lost or stolen in prior 12 months.

## 12.6 Suspicious Circumstance Flags and SIU Referral

Evaluate the following red-flag indicators during intake. Any flag should trigger at minimum a documented narrative in the claim file; multiple flags or a single severe flag trigger mandatory SIU referral per Section 14 (SIU Referral Criteria).

**Theft Report Indicators:**
- Vehicle parked in high-crime area reported by police, yet insured reports being "surprised" by theft despite repeated theft reports in police precinct data.
- No visible signs of forced entry or ignition tampering (see Section 12.7 for detailed inspection).
- Police report indicates vehicle was found unlocked or windows down.
- Vehicle was reported stolen but insured continued to pay monthly finance or lease payments without inquiry about replacement vehicle (suggests knowledge of location).

**Ownership and Financial Indicators:**
- Vehicle financed (loan-to-value > 100% or upside-down loan), suggesting insured is financially motivated for total-loss settlement.
- Recent policy issuance (< 30 days) prior to theft (common fraud pattern).
- Prior theft losses on other vehicles within past 24 months.
- Named insured or household member involved in prior fraud determination at any carrier (check ISO ClaimSearch).

**Timing and Notice Indicators:**
- Theft discovered during off-hours or overnight, making police-report filing delayed.
- Gap between loss discovery and notice to Contoso exceeds 12 hours without reasonable explanation.
- Police report filed before notice to insurance (reverses normal order).
- Insured requests immediate total-loss settlement before investigation can be conducted.

**Narrative Inconsistency Indicators:**
- Insured's description of vehicle condition differs materially from police report (e.g., insured reports "fully locked," police report shows "driver window down").
- Insured cannot describe contents of vehicle (personal items, fuel level, mileage on odometer).
- Insured unclear about where vehicle was parked (uses vague location like "near the mall" instead of specific address or lot).

Document all indicators with specific quotes from insured, police report, or investigative sources. Do not assume guilt, but do refer to SIU if pattern is present.

## 12.7 Forced Entry and Ignition Condition Analysis (Recovered Vehicles)

When a stolen vehicle is recovered, a joint inspection must document:
- **Forced entry points:** Broken or pried door frames, broken windows (location and direction of breaking), steering column damage, ignition tumbler signs of tampering, broken trunk lock.
- **Ignition condition:** Photos of ignition switch showing scratch marks, tool marks, or absence of tumbler; document whether vehicle had keyless entry or traditional key ignition.
- **No-forced-entry scenarios:** If recovered vehicle shows no forced entry and no ignition tampering, document thoroughly in claim file:
  - Multiple photos of all four doors, trunk, hood, and all windows (fronts, backs, sides).
  - Odometer reading and photograph.
  - Key fob found in vehicle (if so, flag immediately for SIU as indicator of insured involvement or non-theft scenario).
  - Interior for signs of violence, distress, or contents unusual for reported vehicle use.
- If no forced entry is found, do not conclude non-theft. Recovered vehicles without forced entry can indicate sophisticated theft (relay attack on keyless-entry vehicles, valet-key theft, insider knowledge). Refer to SIU for assessment.

## 12.8 Documentation Checklist for Theft Claims

**Mandatory at FNOL:**
- Completed Auto Insurance Claim Form (Form CM-AIC-100)
- Police report case number and jurisdiction
- Insured statement with exact location, date, time theft discovered
- Vehicle VIN and policy declarations page

**Mandatory before settlement:**
- Certified copy of police report
- NICB report (printout of database query confirming vehicle status)
- ACV valuation report (for unrecovered vehicles)
- Photos of vehicle (from insured if recovered; from inspection if recovered by police)

**Conditional (depends on circumstances):**
- Evidence of spare keys (if no spare exists, request written statement)
- Vehicle finance documents (loan amount, payoff, LTV calculation)
- Prior loss history report
- Mobile device location data (if consented by insured and SIU requires to establish loss location)

See Section 17 (Documentation Checklist—Comprehensive) for retention requirements and system storage locations.
