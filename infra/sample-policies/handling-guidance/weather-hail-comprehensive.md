# Section 17A - Weather and Hail Comprehensive Claims Handling

This advisory playbook covers comprehensive auto claims arising from severe weather, including hail, windstorm, falling objects, flood, and concurrent glass damage. Use it to guide claim handling after the member-held policy contract has been verified separately.

## 17A.1 Coverage Verification

Before making a coverage recommendation, verify the member-held policy record by exact policy number, confirm the policy was active on the date of loss, and match the reported VIN to a covered auto. Handling guidance cannot establish coverage by itself; it only explains how to manage the claim once the policy facts are known.

## 17A.2 Weather Loss Evidence

For hail or windstorm losses, collect evidence that connects the damage to the reported event and location. Acceptable evidence includes National Weather Service storm reports, local law-enforcement or municipal incident reports, dated photos, repair facility inspection notes, or other reliable records showing hail size, storm timing, and vehicle location.

## 17A.3 Damage Pattern Review

Hail damage should generally show distributed impact dimpling on upward-facing panels such as hood, roof, trunk lid, and upper door edges. Glass damage from hail may include pitting, star breaks, or spreading cracks. If the observed pattern includes directional scraping, bumper deformation, paint transfer, or undercarriage impact, review for collision or another cause of loss.

## 17A.4 Repair Review

Paintless dent repair is appropriate when hail dents are shallow, paint is intact, panel edges are not stretched, and the repair facility confirms access for tooling. Windshield replacement should include ADAS calibration when the vehicle has windshield-mounted cameras or sensors and calibration is required by the repair procedure.

## 17A.5 Decision Guidance

Approve or approve with conditions when the policy is active, the VIN matches a covered auto, comprehensive coverage applies, and weather evidence plus damage pattern are consistent. Investigate further when the policy cannot be found, the VIN does not match, the date of loss falls outside the policy period, weather evidence is missing, or the damage pattern is inconsistent with hail.
