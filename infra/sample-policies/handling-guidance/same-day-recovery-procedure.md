# Section 16 — Same-Day and Near-Term Vehicle Recovery Procedure

This section provides specific handling procedures when a reported stolen vehicle is recovered within 48 hours of first notice of loss (FNOL).

## 16.1 First-Priority Steps Upon Recovery Notification

When Contoso Mutual receives notification that a reported-stolen vehicle has been recovered, the adjuster must immediately establish:

**Recovery Timing:**
- Exact date and time of vehicle recovery (confirm from police or towing company, not just insured report).
- If recovery is within 48 hours of FNOL, invoke this Section 16 protocol immediately.
- If recovery is 48–72 hours post-FNOL, use Section 16 protocol with extended timeline (14 calendar days to settlement, Section 12.3).
- If recovery is beyond 72 hours, treat recovery as delayed; investigate for suspicious circumstances and route to Section 12.4 (Unrecovered Vehicle Workflow) for assessment of staged-theft indicators.

**Recovery Location and Custody:**
- Where was vehicle recovered (police impound, private salvage lot, private party home, street, highway)?
- Who recovered vehicle (police during patrol, citizen report to police, insured personally, towing company)?
- Who currently has custody of vehicle (police evidence hold, municipal impound, private tow lot)?
- Request authorization from police or towing company to conduct inspection at recovery location.

**Notification Chain:**
- Who notified Contoso (police, insured, towing company, salvage pool)?
- Document name, title, phone number of person providing notification.
- Police report number updated to reflect recovery (police often issue supplemental report when stolen vehicle is recovered).

## 16.2 Joint Inspection Requirement

**Critical Protocol:** When a stolen vehicle is recovered within 48 hours of FNOL, a joint inspection must be conducted at the recovery location or at impound facility. Joint inspection means both an adjuster (or trained claim representative) AND a law enforcement representative or salvage professional are present during the inspection.

**Timing:**
- Schedule joint inspection within 24 hours of recovery notification whenever possible.
- If vehicle is in police evidence hold (for criminal investigation), request police priority release for civil inspection.

**Inspection Checklist:**

1. **Exterior Condition:**
   - Photograph all four sides of vehicle, hood, trunk, from multiple angles (at least 4 photos).
   - Document any visible damage (dents, broken glass, missing parts, scratches, paint transfer).
   - Inspect for forced-entry damage: broken windows, pried door frames, damaged trunk lock, scratches around door handles or ignition area.
   - Inspect undercarriage for hit-and-run damage, fluid leaks, or signs of collision.

2. **Interior Condition:**
   - Photograph interior from multiple angles.
   - Check for signs of violence or distress (broken steering wheel, bloodstains, torn upholstery).
   - Inspect for personal items left in vehicle.
   - Odometer reading and photograph (compare to "mileage at theft" reported by insured).

3. **Ignition and Steering Column:**
   - **Critical observation:** Inspect ignition switch for tool marks, damage, or signs of tampering.
   - Photograph ignition area from multiple angles and close-up.
   - Document whether original key will turn ignition or whether new ignition was installed.

4. **Key Recovery:**
   - Check vehicle for keys (glove box, visor, seat cushions, under seat).
   - If insured's original key or spare key is found in recovered vehicle, document location and flag for SIU review.

5. **Vehicle Function and Fluids:**
   - Attempt to start vehicle (if safe to do so and permitted by police/tow lot).
   - Check for fluid leaks.
   - Fuel level at recovery vs. fuel level reported by insured at theft.

## 16.3 Documenting Condition and Damage Assessment

All joint inspection findings must be documented in the claim file with:
- **Inspector name, title, credentials**
- **Inspection date and time**
- **Pre-loss condition reference**
- **Post-recovery condition description**
- **Photo packet** (at least 15–20 photos covering all areas noted above)
- **Assessment of new damage**

## 16.4 Deductible Application for Recovered Vehicles

**Key principle:** Comprehensive coverage deductible applies once per occurrence. For a stolen vehicle recovered and subsequently repaired:

- If theft is the covered loss, comprehensive deductible (e.g., $250) applies to the entire loss (theft + any resulting damage from joy-riding, recovery impound, or otherwise).
- If recovered vehicle is repaired at Contoso-approved shop, deductible is applied at repair approval stage.
- Insured does not pay deductible twice (once for "theft" and once for "repair").

**Example: Same-day recovery with damage:**
- Vehicle stolen and recovered same-day with $5,000 in damage (broken windows, damaged ignition, joy-riding damage).
- Repair estimate: $5,000
- Comprehensive deductible: $250
- Insured cost: $250
- Contoso settlement: $4,750

**If Recovery Results in Total Loss:**
- If recovered vehicle damage exceeds 80% ACV (see Section 11.1), declare total loss.
- Apply comprehensive deductible to total-loss ACV settlement.

## 16.5 Comprehensive Coverage Continuation Pending Repair

If insured wishes to drive recovered vehicle pending repair authorization:

- Advise insured in writing that vehicle has been reported stolen and recovered; safety and reliability are unknown.
- Insured assumes all risk of driving unrepaired recovered vehicle.
- Recommend insured have vehicle inspected by trusted independent mechanic before operating.
- Comprehensive coverage remains in force on vehicle pending repair; however, if vehicle is stolen again, second theft claim is a separate occurrence and subject to separate deductible.

## 16.6 Investigation Window and Settlement Timeline

For same-day and 48-hour recovered vehicles, investigation and settlement timeline is compressed:

**Investigation Period:**
- Standard investigation window per Section 7.3 is 30 calendar days.
- For recovered vehicles, investigation focus is narrowed to: (1) confirmation of theft via police report, (2) documentation of recovery condition via joint inspection, (3) repair estimate and damage assessment.
- All three items should be complete within 10 calendar days of FNOL for same-day recoveries.

**Settlement Timeline:**
- Approval of repair estimate: within 5 business days of estimate receipt.
- Settlement check issuance: within 2 business days of insured confirmation of repair completion and invoice submission.
- **Target: Claim settled within 14 calendar days of FNOL** for same-day recovered vehicles with minor-to-moderate damage.

## 16.7 Police Case Status and Claim Closure

**Coordination with Police:**
- At time of joint inspection, inquire about police case status.
- If case is open, offer cooperation: Contoso can provide claim file documentation, vehicle inspection photos, and access to insured for police statement if needed for criminal investigation.

**Claim Closure:**
- Close claim in standard timeframe (typically within 7 days of settlement check issuance).
- Notation in closed claim file: "Vehicle recovered same-day, repaired [or totaled], loss resolved. Police criminal case [open/closed] as of [date]."

## 16.8 Documentation Checklist for Same-Day Recovered Claims

**Mandatory:**
- Completed FNOL form with insured statement
- Police report (original theft report + recovery supplemental report)
- Recovery notification documentation
- Joint inspection photo packet (15+ photos)
- Joint inspection narrative report
- Repair estimate (or total-loss valuation if damaged beyond repair)

**Conditional:**
- Pre-loss photos (if available from insured)
- Personal items inventory (if items were recovered in vehicle)
- Police case status inquiry note

Retain same-day recovered claim file per Section 17 (Comprehensive Claim Documentation) retention requirements (7 years post-closure).
