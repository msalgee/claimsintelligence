# Section 13 — Glass and Windshield Claims Handling

This section covers the handling of glass-only comprehensive claims and glass-component portions of larger claims, including deductible treatment, repair-vs-replace logic, network routing, and ADAS camera calibration requirements.

## 13.1 Glass Claim Classification

At FNOL, classify the glass claim into one of three categories:

**Standalone Glass Claim:** Comprehensive coverage triggered solely by glass damage (windshield, windows, mirrors, sunroof). Vehicle body, frame, and non-glass systems undamaged. Common causes: rock strike, vandalism to windows only, falling object.

**Glass as Part of Comprehensive Event:** Glass damage occurring concurrently with other comprehensive losses. Examples: theft with broken side window, hail event with windshield pitting and hail dents, vandalism to body and windows together. These claims are rated under the broader comprehensive occurrence and follow the comprehensive claim procedure.

**Glass as Part of Collision:** Windshield or window damage resulting from collision with another vehicle or object (e.g., head-on collision with windshield failure). These follow collision-procedure handling (not covered by this section) and separate deductible.

Confirm coverage tier on policy: Glass coverage may be excluded (coverage denial), subject to standard comprehensive deductible (e.g., $250), or may carry glass-specific deductible (e.g., $0 deductible for repair-not-replace). Document the deductible structure on the claim at FNOL.

## 13.2 Contoso Glass Network and Repair Authorization

Contoso Mutual has established preferred-provider agreements with regional glass shops and mobile-glass operators, collectively referred to as the "Contoso Glass Network."

For standalone glass claims:
- At FNOL, provide insured with a current list of nearby Contoso Glass Network facilities (updated quarterly).
- Advise insured that repairs performed at Contoso Glass Network facilities may have zero deductible or reduced deductible per policy endorsement (e.g., $0 deductible for repair-not-replace at network shops).
- If insured chooses a non-network facility, advise that deductible will apply at full amount (e.g., $250) per policy.
- Authorization for Contoso Glass Network facility repair is streamlined: verify coverage, confirm deductible amount, and issue authorization. Insured is responsible for any amount over policy limits.

For non-network shop choice:
- Require repair estimate from the shop insured selected.
- Verify estimate is reasonable and within market rates for the geographic area (use CCC glass-pricing database).
- If estimate exceeds market-average estimate by more than 15%, request alternate estimate or negotiate repair rate with original shop.
- Approve reasonable estimates and reimburse insured after repair and invoice submission.

## 13.3 Repair vs Replace Decision Logic

**Glass repair should be recommended when:**
- Damage is limited to a single impact point or small area (e.g., bull's-eye, half-moon crack pattern).
- Crack or impact is in the lower portion of windshield (outside driver line-of-sight).
- Vehicle is equipped with sensors or cameras, but the windshield damage does not affect the sensor field of view (see Section 13.5).
- Repair cost is 40% or less of replacement cost.

**Glass replacement should be recommended or required when:**
- Crack runs across driver line-of-sight (center windshield at seated driver eye level).
- Multiple impact points exist or damage is distributed across windshield.
- Windshield laminate is delaminating or showing separation.
- Vehicle is equipped with Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) with camera or sensors behind windshield and damage affects field of view (see Section 13.5).
- Repair would leave distortion or optical aberration in driver field-of-sight (assess via qualified glass technician).

If repair is performed and insured later disputes quality or reports recurring failure, allow one opportunity for re-repair at no cost to insured. If failure recurs, approve replacement at Contoso's cost.

## 13.4 Deductible Buyback Endorsements and Policy Amendments

Many Contoso Mutual comprehensive policies include optional glass deductible buyback (also termed "glass coverage rider" or "zero deductible for glass repair-not-replace"). Confirm presence of endorsement at FNOL:

- If endorsement is present: Insured receives $0 deductible for repair-not-replace at Contoso Glass Network facilities. If replacement is required, full deductible applies. If insured chooses non-network repair, $0 deductible still applies if repair-not-replace logic is met.
- If endorsement is absent: Comprehensive deductible applies to all glass claims (repair or replace).
- If insured requests to add glass deductible buyback mid-claim ("I want to waive my deductible for this claim"), do not approve on claim. Advise insured that endorsement changes are effective on policy renewal or can be added on next renewal. Current claim is governed by endorsements in force at date of loss.

Document the exact endorsement name and form number (e.g., "Contoso Glass Deductible Waiver, Form CM-GLB-12") in the claim file for audit trail.

## 13.5 ADAS Camera and Sensor Calibration Requirements

Modern vehicles increasingly have Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) with cameras, radar, or LIDAR sensors mounted behind the windshield or in bumpers. When windshield replacement or significant body damage repairs occur, these systems may require calibration to maintain proper function (e.g., automatic emergency braking, lane-keeping assist, adaptive cruise control).

**ADAS Calibration Workflow:**

1. **Identify ADAS vehicles:** At FNOL or repair estimate stage, ask insured: "Does your vehicle have automatic emergency braking, lane-keeping assist, or adaptive cruise control?" If yes, treat as ADAS-equipped vehicle. Confirm in vehicle VIN decoder if uncertain.

2. **Determine sensors affected:** If windshield is replaced or bumper is replaced (front or rear), ADAS cameras/sensors may require recalibration.

3. **Require calibration estimate:** Direct repair shop to include ADAS calibration line item in repair estimate if windshield is being replaced or bumper is being removed. Calibration typically costs $150–$500 depending on vehicle and number of cameras.

4. **Approve calibration as part of repair:** Include ADAS calibration in approved repair estimate and authorization. Do not treat calibration as a separate claim or debate with insured.

5. **Post-repair verification:** Request that repair shop perform and document ADAS system self-test or provide calibration report confirming system is within specification after repair.

Failure to recalibrate ADAS after windshield replacement exposes Contoso to liability risk (system malfunction post-repair could lead to injury or property damage if customer assumes system is functional). Always approve calibration when required by repair necessity.

## 13.6 Documentation for Glass Claims

**For standalone glass claims:**
- Photo of damage (dated, showing impact point, crack pattern, any missing glass)
- Repair shop estimate or invoice (if repair is approved and completed)
- Confirmation of deductible applied (network vs non-network)
- Any ADAS calibration report if vehicle is ADAS-equipped

**For glass as part of comprehensive event:**
- See Section 17 (Documentation Checklist—Comprehensive) for full documentation.
- Ensure glass damage photos are distinct from other damage photos.

**Retention:**
- Glass-only claims: retain file 5 years post-closure (shorter than multi-system comprehensive claims).
- Glass as part of comprehensive: retain per comprehensive claim retention (7 years).

## 13.7 Mobile Glass and Rapid Service Options

Contoso Glass Network includes mobile-glass operators that perform on-site repairs (commonly at insured's home or workplace). Mobile repair is preferred for repair-not-replace scenarios because:
- Faster service (same-day or next-day appointments typically available).
- No transportation of vehicle required.
- Urethane cure time allows same-day vehicle use in many cases.

When insured requests mobile-glass service:
- Verify location where repair will be performed (must be weather-protected; outdoor on-street repair is not acceptable for windshield).
- Confirm with mobile operator that weather conditions support repair (extreme cold, rain, high humidity can compromise urethane cure and should delay repair).
- Advise insured of any vehicle use restrictions post-repair (e.g., "do not drive for 2 hours" or "park away from sun for 24 hours").

If weather prevents mobile-glass repair, offer insured option to visit nearby Contoso Glass Network facility for in-shop repair.
