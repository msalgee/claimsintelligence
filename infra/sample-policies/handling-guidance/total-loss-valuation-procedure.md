# Section 15 — Total-Loss Valuation Procedure

This section provides operational procedures for determining actual cash value (ACV) of a total-loss vehicle, including valuation tools, comparable vehicle selection criteria, adjustments, settlement mechanics, and state-specific title-branding requirements.

## 15.1 When to Order Valuation and Valuation Timing

Order an ACV valuation in the following scenarios:

**At Repair Estimate Stage:**
- Repair estimate (first estimate received) is ≥ 70% of estimated vehicle ACV. When this threshold is reached, immediately order formal ACV valuation to confirm whether vehicle will meet total-loss threshold (see Section 11.1: 80% of ACV).
- Do not wait for second estimate to be obtained.

**When Vehicle Cannot Be Located or Repaired:**
- Vehicle is unrecovered after 30-day search period (see Section 12.4).
- Vehicle is recovered but damage is so extensive that no repair estimate can be obtained.

**Timing:**
- Order valuation within 2 business days of determining valuation trigger threshold is met.
- Standard turnaround is 5 business days for online/database valuations; 10 business days for detailed written valuations with comparable vehicle research.

## 15.2 Primary and Secondary Valuation Tools

**Primary Valuation Tool: CCC ONE**
- CCC ONE is Contoso Mutual's primary auto-valuation tool. It accesses national wholesale databases, salvage pool pricing, and auction data to generate vehicle valuations.
- To use CCC ONE: Enter vehicle VIN, mileage at time of loss, and vehicle condition (poor, fair, good, excellent). System returns estimated ACV with low and high range.
- CCC ONE report should be obtained in every total-loss case and documented in claim file.

**Secondary Valuation Tool: JD Power (formerly Kelley Blue Book)**
- Used to validate CCC ONE estimate or to provide alternative estimate if CCC ONE range is too wide.
- Obtain JD Power estimate if CCC ONE range exceeds 25% variance between low and high estimate.
- If CCC ONE and JD Power estimates differ by more than 10%, calculate midpoint and use midpoint as proposed ACV; document difference in claim file.

**Tertiary Valuation: Local Comparable Listings**
- When database valuations seem inconsistent with local market, conduct manual search of local comparable listings (Craigslist, Autotrader, local dealer listings).
- See Section 15.3 (Comparable Vehicle Criteria) for selection requirements.

## 15.3 Comparable Vehicle Selection Criteria

When conducting manual comparable research (tertiary valuation), identify vehicles meeting all of the following criteria:

**Vehicle Match Requirements:**
- Same or adjacent year (within ±1 model year).
- Same make and model.
- Same trim level or adjacent trim (±1 trim level is acceptable).
- Mileage within ±15% of total-loss vehicle mileage.
- Same transmission type and drivetrain.

**Market and Listing Requirements:**
- Sold (completed sale) or listed with asking price within past 90 days.
- Sold by dealer, private party, or auction house (acceptable sources); sales through same salvage pool are not acceptable.
- Located within 75 miles of geographic market where total-loss vehicle is located.
- Asking price or sale price is visible (not "call for price").

**Condition Assessment:**
- Vehicle condition described as "fair," "good," or "excellent" (avoid "poor" or "needs work" comparables).

**Minimum Count:**
- Identify at least 3 comparable vehicles meeting all above criteria.

## 15.4 Adjustments to Comparable Vehicle Values

When using comparable vehicle prices to establish ACV range, apply the following adjustments:

**Mileage Adjustment:**
- Industry standard: $0.10–$0.15 per mile (varies by vehicle age and market).

**Condition Adjustment:**
- If total-loss vehicle was in "excellent" condition before loss and comparable is in "good" condition, add 5% to comparable price.
- If total-loss vehicle was in "fair" or "poor" condition before loss, subtract 5–10% from comparable price.

**Mechanical Systems:**
- If total-loss vehicle has documented recent repairs (engine rebuild, transmission replacement, new brake system, documented within 12 months before loss), add 5–10%.

**Aftermarket Modifications:**
- Quality modifications (documented receipts, professional installation): add 5–15% of modification cost.
- Poor-quality or undocumented modifications: do not add value.

**Sales Tax and Title Fees:**
- After determining vehicle ACV, add estimated sales tax and title transfer fees to reach settlement amount.
- Sales tax: Use state tax rate applicable to loss location.
- Title transfer fee: Add actual title transfer fee charged by state motor vehicle bureau.

## 15.5 Settlement Letter Generation and Lienholder Coordination

When ACV is finalized, generate Settlement Letter (standard form: Form CM-ACP-30, "Total Loss Valuation and Settlement Offer").

Letter must include:
- Vehicle VIN, year/make/model, mileage at time of loss
- Date of loss and date of valuation
- ACV amount (itemized: comparable base price, adjustments, sales tax, title fee)
- Applicable deductible (reference policy page and deductible amount)
- **Settlement amount: ACV less deductible**
- Salvage retention election and deadline (see Section 15.6)
- Reference to Section 11 (Policy clauses on total loss and ACV)
- Insurance adjuster contact information and deadline for insured response (typically 14 calendar days)

If vehicle has a lienholder (loan) or lessor:
- Obtain lienholder payoff statement showing loan balance, accrued interest, and payoff date.
- Calculate settlement amount after payoff: Settlement Amount – Lienholder Payoff = Amount Due to Insured (can be negative if insured is upside-down on loan).
- Coordinate payment: issue settlement check jointly payable to insured and lienholder, or issue separate checks, depending on lienholder requirement.

## 15.6 Salvage Retention Election

**Option A (Most Common): Retain Salvage**
- Insured receives ACV less deductible as settlement amount.
- Contoso Mutual takes ownership of salvaged vehicle and arranges for salvage pool sale or parts dismantling.

**Option B (Less Common): Insured Retains Salvage**
- Insured receives reduced settlement: ACV less deductible less estimated salvage value.
- Salvage value is estimated by licensed salvage pool.

**Election Timeline:**
- Insured has 10 calendar days from receipt of settlement letter to make salvage election.
- If insured does not respond within 10 days, assume salvage retention by Contoso (Option A).

## 15.7 State-Specific Title-Branding Requirements

When a vehicle is declared total loss and Contoso Mutual retains salvage, state law requires the title to be branded:

**New Mexico (NM) Specific:**
- See Section 18 (NM State Amendments) for full NM title-branding requirements and procedures.
- NM requires "Total Loss" or "Salvage" notation on title for any vehicle declared total loss by insurer.

**General US Standards:**
- Many states require "Salvage" title branding when insurer accepts salvage.
- Some states require "Rebuilt" title branding when salvage vehicle is subsequently repaired and returned to road use.

## 15.8 Documentation Checklist for Total-Loss Valuation

**Mandatory:**
- CCC ONE valuation report
- First repair estimate received
- Settlement letter (Form CM-ACP-30) with ACV calculation and salvage election
- Comparable vehicle list (if manual comparables were researched)

**If Lienholder Present:**
- Lienholder payoff statement
- Salvage assignment and release form (Form CM-ACP-31)
- Lienholder notification of settlement and payoff amount

**If Insured Retains Salvage:**
- Form CM-ACP-32 (Salvage Retained by Insured acknowledgment)
- Salvage pool valuation quote used to calculate salvage deduction

Retain total-loss claim file for 7 years post-closure per Section 17 requirements.
