# Section 17 — Documentation Checklist for Comprehensive Claims

This section specifies the required documentation for comprehensive (theft, vandalism, glass, animal, weather, hail) claims at different stages: mandatory at FNOL, mandatory before settlement, conditional, and file retention requirements.

## 17.1 Documentation Mandatory at First Notice of Loss (FNOL)

**Claim Initiation Documents:**
- Completed Auto Insurance Claim Form (Form CM-AIC-100), signed by insured and dated within 30 days of loss.
- Proof of insured identity (copy of driver's license or government-issued ID).
- Policy declarations page (current version on file with Contoso).

**Loss Description and Incident Details:**
- Insured's written or recorded statement describing loss, including: date of loss, time loss discovered, exact location of loss, weather conditions at time of loss, description of damage, actions taken immediately after discovering loss.
- For theft: address where vehicle was parked, whether door was locked, whether windows were up, whether keys were in vehicle.
- For vandalism: description of vandal's actions observed (if insured witnessed) or description of damage patterns.
- For weather/hail: timestamp of weather event, location of vehicle.
- For animal/falling object: description of incident.

**Police or Incident Report (if applicable):**
- Police report case number and jurisdiction (police department name and phone number).
- Adjuster notes confirming police report has been filed.
- For theft, vandalism, and injury: police report is required before claim payment.
- Copy of police report to be obtained within 3 business days of FNOL.

**Contact Information:**
- Insured phone number (primary and alternate).
- Insured email address.
- Name and phone number of any witness to the loss.
- Lienholder or lessor contact information and account number (if vehicle is financed or leased).

## 17.2 Documentation Mandatory Before Settlement

**Verification and Condition Documentation:**
- Police report certified copy (obtained from police department, not insured's printed copy).
- Photographs of all visible damage to vehicle, taken before any repair work begins. Minimum 5 photos (front, rear, both sides, close-up of damage areas). Photos must be dated or timestamped.
- For theft: joint inspection report if recovered (see Section 16.2). For unrecovered theft: NICB and NCIC database reports confirming theft status.
- For vandalism: photos showing vandal's actions.
- For weather/hail: photos showing impact pattern and damage distribution.

**Repair Estimate and Authorization:**
- Completed repair estimate from Contoso-approved or licensed body shop, itemizing parts, labour, and total cost.
- For claims under $7,500 estimated repair: one estimate is required.
- For claims $7,500 or above: two independent estimates are required (from different shops). If estimates vary by more than 15%, settle at lower estimate or require insured to pay difference if insured chooses higher-priced shop.
- Estimate must include labor rate, parts sourcing (OEM, aftermarket, salvage), and warranty on repairs.

**Vehicle Ownership and Insurance Verification:**
- Title/ownership documents confirming Contoso-insured is the registered owner.
- Current vehicle registration (copy on file or verification via state DMV).
- For total-loss vehicles: lienholder payoff statement (if vehicle is financed).

**Claim-Specific Documentation by Loss Type:**
- **Glass claims:** See Section 13.6.
- **Theft claims:** See Section 12.8.
- **Same-day recovered theft:** See Section 16.8.
- **Total-loss claims:** See Section 15.8.

## 17.3 Conditional Documentation (Loss-Type and Value Dependent)

**Medical Records and Injury Documentation:**
- Required: If claim includes injury to insured or passenger, request medical records for any emergency room or urgent care visit related to loss.
- NOT required: For comprehensive-only claims with no injury.

**Financial and Prior-Loss Documentation:**
- Required: For claims exceeding $25,000 estimated value, obtain ISO ClaimSearch report for named insured and spouse to identify prior loss history.
- Required: For total-loss claims, obtain NICB report on vehicle and lienholder payoff statement.
- NOT required: For routine claims under $10,000 with no fraud indicators.

**Vehicle Modification and Aftermarket Equipment Documentation:**
- Required: If vehicle contains documented aftermarket modifications and claim value exceeds $15,000, request insured to provide receipts or photographic evidence.
- Required: If insured claims personal property inside vehicle was damaged or destroyed, request itemized list and photographs if possible.

**State-Specific Documentation:**
- Required for NM claims: See Section 18 (NM State Amendments) for state-specific forms and procedures.

**SIU Investigation Documentation:**
- Required: If claim is referred to SIU, SIU will specify additional documentation needs (witness interviews, EUO, salvage inspection, etc.). See Section 14.

## 17.4 Missing or Unavailable Documentation

If required documentation is not available or insured is unable to provide, adjuster has the following options:

**Extension Request:**
- Insured has 10 calendar days from request to provide missing item.
- Issue written request to insured (email or letter) specifying the missing item and deadline.

**Alternative Documentation:**
- If insured cannot provide photos, accept written description from insured or adjuster-conducted inspection in person.
- If police report is not available, proceed to settlement once reasonable time has passed (typically 7–10 business days).

**Partial Settlement or Repairs:**
- Issue partial authorization to begin repairs while outstanding documentation is gathered.

**Information Gaps and Claim Denial:**
- If critical documentation is genuinely unavailable and loss facts cannot be verified, adjuster may deny claim for lack of cooperation (per policy cooperation clause).

## 17.5 Claims System Documentation Storage

**Document Organization by Claim Stage:**

1. **FNOL Documents:** Claim form, insured statement, police report case number, contact information list
2. **Investigation Documents:** Police report (certified copy), NICB/NCIC reports, inspection photos, witness interviews, adjuster narrative notes
3. **Settlement Documents:** Repair estimates, repair shop approvals, total-loss valuation reports, lienholder correspondence, settlement offer letter
4. **Closure Documents:** Final repair invoice or total-loss settlement acceptance, settlement check confirmation, claim closure letter to insured

**Digital Image Requirements:**
- All photos/images uploaded to claims system must be tagged with date, location, and description.
- Medical records, financial documents, and sensitive personal information must be stored in secure document folder (not public claim note area).

## 17.6 File Retention Requirements

**By Claim Status:**
- **Closed claim, no issues:** Retain 5 years from claim closure date.
- **Closed claim with SIU involvement or prior fraud history on insured:** Retain 7 years from claim closure date.
- **Denied claim or claim with litigation potential:** Retain 7 years from denial date or litigation conclusion date, whichever is later.
- **Total-loss claim:** Retain 7 years from settlement date.

**Audit and Record-Keeping Holds:**
- If claim is selected for audit by regulatory authority, retain file until audit is concluded and any remediation is complete.
- Files involved in litigation must be retained per litigation hold instructions.

## 17.7 Documentation Audit and Quality Check

**Audit Triggers:**
- Random quality review (sample 5–10% of closed claims per month).
- Claims exceeding $20,000 settlement (mandatory manager review before closure).
- Claims involving SIU referral or fraud indicators (mandatory legal review before closure).

**Audit Checklist:**
- Is FNOL documentation complete?
- Are required repair estimates or valuation reports present?
- Are photos or inspection reports present?
- Are copies of settlement offer and acceptance documents in file?
- Is claim closure summary present?
- Are sensitive documents properly secured/restricted?

Comprehensive documentation standards ensure that claims can be audited, explained, and defended if questioned by insured, regulator, or court.
