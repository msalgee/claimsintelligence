# Section 18 — New Mexico State-Specific Procedures and Regulatory Amendments

This section incorporates New Mexico Insurance Code requirements, unfair claims practice prohibitions, prompt-payment regulations, title-branding rules, and language-access requirements that modify or supplement the general claims procedures in Sections 7–17. When NM-specific requirements conflict with general procedures, NM requirements control for NM-domiciled insureds and NM-situs losses.

## 18.1 NM Insurance Code Prompt-Payment Timeline Requirements

New Mexico Insurance Code §59A-16-1 specifies mandatory timelines for claim acknowledgment, investigation decisions, and payment:

**Acknowledgment Timeline:**
- Contoso Mutual must acknowledge a properly submitted claim in writing within **15 calendar days** of receiving notice of loss.
- "Properly submitted" means FNOL with sufficient information for Contoso to begin investigation (insured name, date of loss, type of loss, vehicle description).
- Acknowledgment letter must include claim number, assigned adjuster name and contact information, anticipated investigation timeline, and statement of Contoso's investigation rights.

**Investigation Decision Timeline:**
- Contoso Mutual must issue a coverage decision (approval or denial) within **30 calendar days** of receiving all required documentation.
- "Required documentation" is specified in Section 7.2 (policy requirements) or claim-specific documentation sections.
- If documentation is incomplete at day 30, Contoso may request extension (see Section 18.2, Extension Procedures).
- Coverage decision must be issued in writing and must specify: (1) whether claim is approved or denied, (2) if approved, settlement amount and breakdown, (3) if denied, specific reason for denial and applicable policy provision, (4) appeal/complaint procedures.

**Payment Timeline:**
- If claim is approved, Contoso Mutual must issue settlement payment within **10 calendar days** of the coverage decision.
- Payment is considered "issued" when check is mailed or electronic transfer is initiated (not when received by insured).
- Settlement check must be payable to insured (and lienholder, if vehicle is financed).

**Example Timeline for NM Claim:**
- Day 0: FNOL phone call (May 1, 9 AM)
- Day 15: Acknowledgment letter mailed (May 16)
- Day 28: All documentation received from insured and police report obtained
- Day 30: Coverage decision issued, $4,500 approved (May 31)
- Day 40: Settlement check issued (June 10)

If investigation requires SIU involvement, the investigation window may be tolled (see Section 18.3).

## 18.2 Extension of Investigation Period

**Extension Request Requirements:**
- Adjuster must send written communication to insured before day 30 expires, requesting an extension.
- Extension request letter must specify: reason investigation cannot be completed by day 30, anticipated number of additional days needed, specific items remaining to complete investigation, insured's right to appeal to NM Insurance Commissioner if extension is unreasonable.
- Extension is not automatic; insured may refuse extension and demand decision within original 30 days.

**SIU Investigation Extension:**
- If claim is referred to SIU (Section 14), investigation timeline may be extended up to 60 calendar days from SIU initiation.
- Contoso must notify insured in writing of SIU involvement and extended timeline within 5 business days of SIU referral.
- At conclusion of SIU investigation, adjuster has 10 additional calendar days to issue coverage decision.

**Violation Penalties:**
- If Contoso Mutual issues decision or payment after deadlines without approved extension, insured may file complaint with NM Insurance Commissioner.
- Violations can result in state regulatory penalties and requirement to pay interest on delayed payments.

## 18.3 NM Unfair Claims Practices Act (NMSA 1978, §59A-16-1 et seq.)

NM law prohibits specific practices in claims handling. Contoso Mutual adjusters must be aware of and avoid these prohibited practices:

**Prohibited Practices:**

1. **Misrepresenting Policy Terms:** Adjuster must not misstate coverage, limits, deductibles, or policy conditions in order to discourage claim or reduce settlement. If policy language is ambiguous, resolve ambiguity in favor of insured (contra proferentem rule).

2. **Compelling Submission of Examinations Under Oath (EUO) Unreasonably:** Adjuster may request EUO to investigate inconsistencies or fraud suspicions, but request must be made in writing with specific reason.

3. **Failing to Acknowledge Communication:** All written communications from insured or insured's representative must be acknowledged in writing within 10 business days.

4. **Settling Identical Claims on Different Bases:** Discrimination in settlement amounts must be justified and documented.

5. **Misrepresenting Policy Provisions:** Adjuster must provide accurate policy information.

6. **Unreasonable Delays in Processing:** Investigations must proceed diligently; unreasonable delays violate UCPA.

7. **Refusing to Provide Written Explanation of Denial:** If claim is denied, insured has the right to written explanation citing specific policy provision and reason for denial.

**Adjuster Accountability:**
- Adjusters who knowingly violate UCPA provisions may be personally liable for damages and subject to disciplinary action.

## 18.4 NM Total-Loss Title-Branding and Salvage Handling

**NM Title-Branding Requirements:**
- NM Motor Vehicle Department must be notified of total-loss determination by insurer.
- Insurer must submit total-loss notice to NM MVD within 30 calendar days of settlement.
- Title will be branded with "Total Loss Vehicle" or "Salvage" notation.
- Branded title must be transferred to salvage purchaser; non-branded title cannot be transferred for total-loss vehicle.

**NM Salvage Pool Requirements:**
- Salvage vehicle must be offered to NM-licensed salvage pool before being sold to out-of-state buyer.
- Contoso must obtain written quote from local NM salvage pool and retain documentation of quote in claim file.

**NM Rebuilt Title and Re-Registration:**
- If salvage vehicle is repaired and owner wants to re-register it, vehicle must be inspected by NM MVD and titled as "Rebuilt".
- Rebuilder is responsible for NM MVD inspection and rebuilt title application.

## 18.5 Sales Tax and Title-Fee Handling for NM Total-Loss Settlements

**Sales Tax on Settlement:**
- NM Gross Receipts Tax rate: approximately 5.125% statewide (rate may vary slightly by locality).
- For total-loss settlement, sales tax is calculated on ACV (before deductible) and added to settlement.
- Example: ACV $20,000 × 5.125% GRT = $1,025 sales tax. Settlement = $20,000 ACV + $1,025 tax = $21,025, less $250 deductible = $20,775 due to insured.

**NM Title Transfer Fee:**
- NM MVD title transfer fee for salvage transfer is approximately $25–$40.
- Fee is added to settlement offer similar to sales tax.

**Documentation:**
- Settlement letter (Form CM-ACP-30) must itemize sales tax and title fee separately.

## 18.6 Language Access Requirements

**Spanish-Language Access (Mandatory for NM):**
- If insured requests FNOL, coverage explanation, or settlement discussion in Spanish, Contoso Mutual must provide interpretation at no cost to insured.
- Contoso must use qualified interpreter (not family member, not untrained employee).
- All written correspondence to Spanish-language insured must be translated to Spanish.
- Forms must be available in Spanish or translated for Spanish-language insured upon request.

**Other Languages:**
- While NM does not mandate interpretation in languages other than Spanish, Contoso should provide interpretation in other languages if practical.

**Adjuster Training:**
- All NM-based adjusters must complete language-access training annually.
- Bilingual adjusters should be utilized where possible for Spanish-language claims.
