# Section 19 — Fraud Indicator Playbook and Response Protocol

This section provides a concrete catalogue of fraud and misrepresentation indicators observed in comprehensive insurance claims. Each indicator is categorized by severity (Low, Medium, High) and recommended response action. This playbook is a decision-support tool for adjusters; presence of one low-severity indicator does not trigger SIU referral, but multiple indicators or any high-severity indicator warrants escalation per Section 14.

## 19.1 Document-Level Fraud Indicators

**Indicator: Missing Police Report Case Number or Report Unavailable**
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Observation:** Insured reports theft or vandalism but is unable to provide police report case number and police department confirms no report was filed.
- **Action:** Issue extension request requiring police report within 5 business days. If insured still cannot produce report after extension, escalate to SIU for investigation or proceed to denial for lack of cooperation.

**Indicator: Repair Estimate Contains Inflated or Fabricated Line Items**
- **Severity:** High
- **Observation:** Repair estimate includes parts or labor that do not match damage photos or police report.
- **Action:** Mandatory SIU referral. Contact repair shop directly to discuss estimate line items. Do not pay inflated estimate without SIU clearance.

**Indicator: Photo EXIF Metadata Date Inconsistent with Reported Loss Date**
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Observation:** Damage photos provided by insured show EXIF date that differs from reported loss date.
- **Action:** Question insured about date discrepancy. Request statement from insured confirming when photos were taken. If explanation is not reasonable, request independent inspection before approving settlement.

**Indicator: Repair Shop Not Registered with State or Has Multiple Regulatory Violations**
- **Severity:** Medium–High
- **Observation:** Repair shop provided by insured does not appear in state licensing database, has been flagged by state for unlicensed operation, or has multiple FTC complaints.
- **Action:** Do not approve estimates from unlicensed or flagged shops. Direct insured to select Contoso-approved or state-licensed shop.

**Indicator: Vehicle Title History Shows Prior Salvage or Rebuilt Title**
- **Severity:** High
- **Observation:** Vehicle was previously titled as "Salvage," "Rebuilt," "Branded," or "Flood-Damaged" and insured does not mention prior loss history.
- **Action:** Mandatory SIU referral and mandatory detailed inspection. Do not settle without detailed forensic inspection confirming current loss did not involve prior-damage areas.

## 19.2 Narrative-Level Fraud Indicators

**Indicator: Loss Description Does Not Match Damage Pattern**
- **Severity:** Medium–High
- **Observation:** Insured's narrative of loss mechanism does not match repair estimate or damage photos.
- **Action:** Document specific mismatches. Request detailed explanation from insured. If explanation is not reasonable, escalate to SIU and request detailed inspection by accident reconstructionist.

**Indicator: Conflicting Statements from Same Insured on Different Dates**
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Observation:** Insured provides different version of loss on FNOL call vs. subsequent communication.
- **Action:** Question insured on discrepancy calmly and professionally. Document response. If insured cannot explain discrepancy or gets defensive, escalate to SIU.

**Indicator: Conflicting Statements Between FNOL and Police Report**
- **Severity:** Medium–High
- **Observation:** Facts in insured's FNOL statement differ materially from facts in police report (taken by police officer from independent sources/witness statements).
- **Action:** Request explanation from insured on specific discrepancy. If explanation reveals material misrepresentation, escalate to SIU for investigation of fraud.

**Indicator: Witness Phone Numbers or Addresses Appear Fictitious or Out of Area**
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Observation:** Witness contact information provided by insured consists of phone numbers that are not in operation or addresses in different state from loss location.
- **Action:** Attempt to contact witness by phone and email. Document contact attempts. If witness is unreachable, consider whether claim can be settled without witness corroboration.

**Indicator: Witness is Related to Insured or Has Financial Interest in Settlement**
- **Severity:** Low–Medium
- **Observation:** Witness is family member of insured or person with financial interest (repair shop owner, salvage buyer).
- **Action:** Document witness relationship in claim file. Contact independent parties to cross-verify facts.

## 19.3 Behavioral Fraud Indicators

**Indicator: Insured Refuses to Provide Recorded Statement or Examination Under Oath**
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Observation:** Insured refuses to participate without providing reasonable explanation.
- **Action:** Issue written communication to insured explaining that cooperation is required by policy and law. If insured continues to refuse after written request, coordinate with SIU on claim closure approach.

**Indicator: Insured Pressure to Settle Quickly or Avoids Detailed Discussion**
- **Severity:** Low–Medium
- **Observation:** Insured repeatedly asks when settlement check will be issued, declines to discuss details of loss, requests payment before investigation is complete.
- **Action:** Explain to insured that investigation and documentation process requires time per policy and law. Provide realistic settlement timeline.

**Indicator: Insured Solicits Adjuster's Help to Inflate or Fabricate Claim**
- **Severity:** High
- **Observation:** Insured directly asks adjuster to include unauthorized items in settlement, suggests inflating repair estimate, or requests adjuster to exclude items from insurance company's knowledge.
- **Action:** Mandatory refusal by adjuster. Document conversation with specific quotes in claim narrative. Report to adjuster supervisor and SIU same day.

## 19.4 Pattern-Level Fraud Indicators

**Indicator: Insured Has Multiple Claims Within Short Timeframe (Frequency Pattern)**
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Observation:** Insured (or household member) has 3 or more covered claims within 18 months.
- **Action:** Query ISO ClaimSearch database to retrieve insured's prior claim history. Escalate to SIU for pattern analysis if frequency increases.

**Indicator: Prior Similar Loss Within 24 Months**
- **Severity:** Medium–High
- **Observation:** Insured reports loss of similar type to prior reported loss within past 24 months.
- **Action:** Mandatory SIU referral. Retrieve prior loss file and compare details.

**Indicator: Insured Involved in Prior Fraud Determination at Any Carrier**
- **Severity:** High
- **Observation:** ISO ClaimSearch database query reveals that insured was subject to fraud determination at another insurance carrier in past 5 years.
- **Action:** Mandatory SIU referral immediately upon discovery of prior fraud history. Do not pay claim until SIU clears.

**Indicator: Third-Party Named in Claim Has Prior Loss History Suggesting Organized Ring**
- **Severity:** High
- **Observation:** Repair shop, salvage buyer, witness, or other third party named in current claim has been associated with multiple other claims at Contoso Mutual or other carriers.
- **Action:** Mandatory SIU and Law Enforcement referral. Do not settle claim until SIU coordinates with law enforcement and other carriers to investigate ring.

## 19.5 Recommended Actions by Indicator Severity

**Low-Severity Indicators (Individual):**
- Action: Document in claim file. Monitor for additional indicators. Proceed with normal investigation and settlement if no other flags are present.

**Medium-Severity Indicators (One or Two Present):**
- Action: Escalate to SIU for consultation. Proceed with settlement only after SIU clearance or guidance.
- Two or more medium-severity indicators = mandatory SIU investigation.

**High-Severity Indicators (Any One Present):**
- Action: Mandatory immediate SIU investigation and escalation. Halt settlement processing.

**Behavioral Refusal to Cooperate or Solicitation of Adjuster Collaboration in Fraud:**
- Action: Immediate escalation to SIU and adjuster supervisor. Document specific conversations.

## 19.6 Documentation and Escalation Protocol

When fraud indicators are identified, adjuster must document and escalate as follows:

1. **Claim Narrative:** Add detailed section titled "Fraud Indicators Identified" and list each indicator with severity level and specific observation.

2. **SIU Referral Form (Form CM-SIU-50):** Complete referral form specifying each indicator as reason for referral.

3. **Escalation Email:** Send email to SIU case manager with claim number, insured name, policy number, summary of indicators, and request for investigation direction.

4. **Supervisor Notification:** Copy adjuster supervisor on escalation email.

5. **Insured Communication:** If claim is placed on hold pending SIU investigation, send professional communication to insured explaining delay without accusing insured of fraud.

Fraud indicator identification and escalation is adjuster's professional obligation under law and company policy.
