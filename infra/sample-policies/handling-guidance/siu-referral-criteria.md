# Section 14 — Special Investigations Unit (SIU) Referral Criteria and Workflow

This section identifies when a claim must be referred to Contoso Mutual's Special Investigations Unit, the referral process, investigative authority and limitations, and communication protocols during SIU involvement.

## 14.1 Mandatory SIU Referral Triggers

The following conditions mandate immediate referral to SIU. Do not delay referral pending additional documentation; refer immediately and note the specific trigger(s) in the referral memo.

**Claim Amount and Inconsistency:**
- Any claim exceeding $25,000 estimated repair or settlement value where any of the following inconsistencies exist: police report facts conflict with insured's statement, repair damage pattern does not match reported loss mechanism (e.g., hail claim with isolated dents instead of distributed impact), estimated vehicle ACV exceeds known market value by 20% or more.

**Prior Loss Pattern:**
- Insured (named insured, spouse, household adult, or dependent) reported a similar claim (theft, total loss, injury) within 24 months of current claim date.
- Named insured or spouse has involvement in a prior fraud determination at any insurer (check ISO ClaimSearch database within 24 hours of FNOL).
- Vehicle being claimed has been reported stolen, damaged, or totaled previously within past 36 months (check NICB, NCIC, and state auto title database).

**Notice and Timing Issues:**
- Police report was filed after notice to Contoso (normal order is notice to police first, then notice to insurer).
- Notice to Contoso was delayed more than 30 days after loss discovery without reasonable explanation.
- Insured is unable or unwilling to explain the delay.

**Statements and Documentation Conflicts:**
- FNOL statement by insured materially conflicts with facts in police report, witness statements, or repair estimate.
- Multiple versions of loss narrative provided by insured on different dates or to different company representatives.
- Insured or witness contact information is missing, false, or deliberately vague.

**Staged Loss or Fraud Indicators:**
- Multiple vehicles owned by insured or household member are covered by Contoso and have claims within 12 months.
- Repair estimate provided by a body shop shows missing or fabricated line items, inconsistent with damage photos or police report.
- Salvage pool cannot locate recovered vehicle at reported impound or tow location.
- Police report indicates possible involvement of insured (e.g., police suspect arson, insured is property owner).

**Total Loss and Salvage Issues:**
- ACV valuation tools show wide variance in value (e.g., CCC ONE shows $18,000 but comparable listings show $12,000–$14,000 for same vehicle).
- Vehicle has been reported as salvage title in any state and insured is claiming it as a normal title.
- Lienholder payoff amount exceeds ACV by more than 15%.

**Arson or Intentional Acts:**
- Loss is total loss by fire and vehicle location is known arson-risk location.
- Insured has no reasonable explanation for vehicle location at time of loss.
- Prior fire or theft claims exist on same vehicle or other vehicles.

## 14.2 Discretionary SIU Referral Indicators

The following are not mandatory triggers but warrant escalation to SIU for consultation or investigation if two or more indicators co-occur in a single claim:

- Insured requests quick settlement before investigation can be completed.
- Insured is unwilling to provide recorded statement or Examination Under Oath (EUO).
- Vehicle has extensive modifications (high-value audio/electronics) that increase replacement value but insured cannot document purchase receipts.
- Total loss threshold is marginal (estimate is 75–85% of ACV, creating ambiguity about repair-vs-total-loss decision).
- Insured receives frequent glass claims (5+ glass claims in 36-month period) across different vehicles or same vehicle.
- Repair shop estimate comes from a shop with no online presence, not listed with local business bureaus, or has FTC complaints in public database.
- Insured changes story regarding vehicle condition after repair has begun.

## 14.3 SIU Referral Process and Documentation

To refer a claim to SIU:

1. **Complete SIU Referral Form (Form CM-SIU-50):** Document the reason(s) for referral, citing specific mandatory triggers or discretionary indicators.

2. **Assemble claim packet:**
   - Completed FNOL claim form and all insured statements
   - Police report (certified copy if available)
   - All repair estimates received to date
   - Photos of damage and vehicle
   - Any prior loss history from ISO ClaimSearch or NICB report
   - Email correspondence with insured, police, or repair shops
   - Adjuster's narrative notes documenting reason for escalation

3. **Submit to SIU electronically** via the claims system SIU queue or dedicated escalation email.

4. **Document submission in claim file** with SIU confirmation receipt date and SIU file number assigned.

5. **Do not close the claim while SIU investigation is pending.** SIU investigation can take 10–90 days. Status on claim should be set to "Under SIU Investigation" and updated weekly if possible.

## 14.4 Investigative Authority During SIU Review

**SIU Authority:**
- SIU investigators may conduct interviews with insured, witnesses, police, repair shops, and salvage pools without prior notice.
- SIU may request that insured sign a consent form allowing SIU to obtain medical records, employment records, or financial records related to the claim.
- SIU may perform surveillance of insured or vehicle (where permitted by state law).
- SIU may request that insured submit to an Examination Under Oath (EUO). Refusal to comply with EUO request may be grounds for coverage denial under cooperation clauses in policy.

**Adjuster Limitations During SIU Review:**
- Standard adjuster should not contact insured directly while investigation is active unless SIU explicitly approves.
- Adjuster should not commit to claim payment, settlement amount, or approval of repairs while SIU investigation is pending.
- Adjuster should not discuss SIU investigation status or findings with insured.

## 14.5 Investigation Window and Tolling

Contoso Mutual's standard investigation window (see Section 7.3) is 30 calendar days from receipt of complete documentation. When a claim is referred to SIU, this window is tolled (paused):

- **Tolling date:** When SIU referral is submitted to SIU queue.
- **Tolling duration:** Standard SIU investigation period is up to 60 days from SIU file creation date.
- **Investigation window resumes:** When SIU investigation concludes and claim is returned to standard adjuster.
- **Final decision deadline:** Standard adjuster has 10 calendar days after SIU conclusion to issue coverage decision.

Always confirm tolling timelines are compliant with applicable state law (see Section 18 for NM-specific tolling rules).

## 14.6 Communication Restrictions During SIU Investigation

If insured requests update on claim status while under SIU investigation, adjuster response is limited to: "Your claim is being reviewed by our Special Investigations Unit. You will receive an update on coverage decision within [X days]. An SIU case manager will contact you if additional information is needed."

SIU investigator will contact police, repair shops, and witnesses directly. Adjuster should not independently contact these parties if claim is under SIU investigation.

## 14.7 SIU Outcomes and Next Steps

When SIU investigation concludes, claim will be returned to adjuster with one of the following recommendations:

**No Fraud Identified:** Proceed to settlement based on coverage analysis.

**Fraud Indicated, Coverage Denied:** Adjuster issues coverage denial letter citing specific basis for denial. Denials based on fraud require senior management approval before issuance.

**Fraud Suspected, Evidence Insufficient for Denial:** Adjuster may approve claim but with adverse notation in claim file, request additional documentation from insured before approving, or refer to claim analytics team for pattern monitoring.

**Coverage Conditional on Insured Cooperation:** SIU requires insured to comply with specific requests (e.g., additional medical records, EUO, salvage inspection) before claim can be approved.

Document all SIU outcomes in claim file with SIU file number, investigation conclusion date, and adjuster's final coverage decision with SIU recommendation.
